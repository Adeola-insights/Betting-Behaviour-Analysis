# Betting-Behaviour-Analysis
This project analyzes betting behavior and addiction patterns among Nigerians using a simulated betting dataset.
# OVERVIEW
 This project analyzes betting behavior and addiction patterns among Nigerians using a simulated betting dataset. The goal is to uncover behavioral trends, financial risk factors, and demographic patterns associated with betting addiction, and to present these insights through an interactive Excel dashboard.
The dashboard is designed to support data-driven discussions around responsible betting, financial impact, and social consequences.
# DATASET DESCRIPTION
The dataset contains individual-level betting behavior and impact indicators, including:

* ID – Unique identifier for each bettor

* Age Group – Youth (18–25), Adult (26–40), Old (41–60)

* Gender – Male, Female

* Avg Weekly Bets – Average number of bets per week

* Avg Bet Amount – Average monetary value per bet

* Monthly Income – Estimated monthly income

* % Income Spent on Betting – Portion of income spent on betting

* Hours Spent on Betting per Week – Time investment in betting activities

* Addiction Level (1–10) – Self-reported betting addiction score

* Most Used Betting App – Primary betting platform

* Has Financial Debt – Indicates if betting has led to debt

* Affected Work Life – Impact of betting on work performance

* Relationship Strain – Impact of betting on personal relationships

* Years Betting – Duration of betting behavior

* Awareness of Risk – Awareness of betting-related risks

* Stopped Betting Before – Previous attempts to stop betting

* Location – City of residence
